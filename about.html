Key milestones

Scroll inside the panel to explore our journey.

1968

Founded in Reggio Emilia

The Company was founded in 1968, and at that time the first products were mainly submersible motors and spare parts for submersible pumps.

1974

Launch of cast-iron submersible line

The real breakthrough began by introducing to the market the first line of submersible pumps made of cast iron and submersible motors with powers up to 100 hp.

1982

Range expanded to 12″ / 300 hp

Continued with the expansion of the range to 12" pumps with powers up to 300 hp.

1988

Global expansion across EMEA

Thanks to the acquisition of important professionals such as qualified engineers and foreign marketing managers, VAMA's market and field of activity expanded in European countries, Middle East, and established its steady presence also in Maghreb, North and Central Africa.

1994

Switch to stainless-steel design

Sensing the opportunity offered by new technologies, the Ownership decided to start the production of stainless steel submersible pumps starting a new design phase from zero.

1995

First 4″ & 6″ steel pumps released

Began the production of the first series of 4" & 6" stainless steel pumps, immediately enjoying unexpected success.

2011

Turn-key stations & surface pumps

Underwent further development by initiating a study of design and construction of complete pumping stations and horizontal mono and multistage pumps.

Recent Developments

Radial / semi-axial series & SCADA

Developed a new series of radial and semi-axial pumps (8", 10", & 12") with powers up to 250 kW.
Introduced new product lines such as Ram Antibody Assemblies, various types of piping and valves, and SCADA management software for full automation of pumping stations.

How we build a pump

Answers to your most-asked production questions.

Which stainless-steel grades do you use?

Standard parts are AISI 304; aggressive fluids get AISI 316L, Duplex or Super-Duplex on request.

Is every pump tested before shipment?

Yes—each unit runs on our ISO 9906 Grade-2B test loop and ships with its own performance curve.

Can you customise flanges or couplings?

Laser-cutting and CNC machining let us swap NEMA/IEC standards or add OEM branding within 48 h.
